Product

Amazon Chime—AWS’s video conferencing and chat service

Goal

Support surge in remote meetings & upgrade overall UX

Role

Define & deliver mobile/tablet scope of redesign

Audience

Knowledge workers that thrive on schedule flexibility for casual calls (e.g. join stand-up while taking kids to school) or periods requiring high availability (e.g. on-call)

Consequent to COVID-19 lockdowns, the Amazon Chime team tackled sweeping upgrades to accomodate the significant surge of remote meetings.

The product was not optimized for the sudden shift in meeting dynamics (being largely unchanged since its acquisition as "Biba" in 2016). We started to understand the new normal, and the team quested to overhaul services from back-end to front-end under a "5.0" release. The modernizations would accelerate future productivity add-ons, support additional use cases, and get the product up to accessibility standards.

I focused on meeting these goals for our mobile/tablet users. I also saw an opportunity to build a more customer-centric process and improve known issues with cross-team communication and efficiency.

Product

Amazon Payments—Global transaction processing

Goal

Accept cash for purchases

Role

Lead designer across desktop, mobile, web

Audience

Under- & un-banked international shoppers that want access to Amazon.com goods, despite lack of access to credit cards

During 2017's rapid company expansion, the Amazon Payments org endeavored to serve a greater swathe of shoppers and accept more payment methods—like cash.

While it may seem surprising for e-commerce, physical currency still has a strong influence on purchasing behaviors across the world, and it's considerably cheaper to process than credit cards. Many countries like Brazil, Mexico, and Indonesia already have voucher-like payments enmeshed in their economy.

I was tasked with integrating this complex method into the byzantine workings of Amazon's checkout process. The project was the first of its kind in the now larger, freshly restructured org; it served as a guinea pig for UX integration, as well as more broadly figuring out how to work together and across other teams at Amazon.

Product

TASL suite—Education support for non-traditional undergrads

Goal

Boost academic success of new students via free mobile apps

Role

Design iOS apps from scratch & monitor analytics

Audience

American students (mostly over 24) that—in some combo—work full time, have dependents, and/or attend school part-time

All Star Directories sought consultants in building a series of mobile apps to support new students and improve institutional retention rates.

For adults returning to school, relearning how to be a student is a daunting obstacle on top of life's other demands. Drop-out rates for the first semester alone is the highest. The ambition of TASL was to improve outcomes for new students through their first quarter, first year, and hopefully to graduation.

From the ground up, I got a chance to design Study Shift—a study tracking app—and Reading Comprehension—an app that teaches and reinforces critical reading skills. With just 3 people, our team ran like a hackathon and made incredible progress within 6 months.

Tech, by definition, is elementally human. Whether it's Paleolithic flint for a warming fire or a modern phone search for a new career, it's about making life better for people.

My philosophy is that research, intentional design, and collaborative teams are they keys to unlocking happy, long-term users. If you feel similarly, let's connect on LinkedIn.
