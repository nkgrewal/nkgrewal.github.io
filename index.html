Following 2020's COVID-19 lockdowns, Amazon Chime undertook sweeping, rapid product improvements to accomodate significant conference call demands.

Our dedicated team coordinated front-end, API, and back-end service overhauls for web, desktop, and mobile apps under the umbrella of a "5.0" release.

Although we lacked definition for how to get there, the end goal was to support larger daily meeting demands and structurally create room for potential productivity add-ons. My focus was meeting these goals for users on mobile/tablet devices.

From the ground up, I got the chance to design Study Shift, a study tracking app, and Reading Comprehension, an app that teaches and reinforces reading skills.

Our team ran like hackathon with 3 people— a PM, an engineer, and myself.

All Star Directories sought consultants to build a suite of speculative mobile apps. For adults returning to school, rediscovering and developing fundamentals of being a student is often challenging. Drop-out rate for the first semester alone is highest. The TASL project had the ambition to build a suite of apps that improve outcomes of new students through their first quarter, first year, and even hopefully to graduation.
