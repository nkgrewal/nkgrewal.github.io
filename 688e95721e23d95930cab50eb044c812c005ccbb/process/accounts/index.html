Design Process

This project was one of the Pro product's more process-intensive UX changes at the time. It threaded into several large-scale projects like the move to SSO (single sign-on), Developer API launch, and a viral marketing campaign moving targeted users from consumer site to Pro.

Original settings

The initial scope was a reskin to match our new branding. Screens below give an overview of Account Settings, Site Administration, and Subscription Plans for purchase.

I also had designed the new Pro API Developer portal. While clean and approachable, we needed to find a more seamless way to integrate it with the rest of our Pro services.

First attempt

While giving the account settings page a refresh, we discovered deeper usability issues. Important existential questions came up like:

  • "Who uses all these sections?"
  • "Who on the acount manages users?"
  • "What do users actually do in account settings?"
  • "What do first time users expect to do when they go into it?"
  • "What don't they use in it?"

Step back - research

Interviews with customers and internal users of Pro revealed areas that needed further analysis, which we pursued with surveys and in-person interviews. I also went along to sales and customer meetings with the lead PM to ask specific usability questions and watch users interact in real-time. Between product and engineering, we had collaborative discussions to suss out goals for redefining account structure as well as potential engineering scope. With that info, I began to map out our collective research and conclusions to see what would make sense for users.

Survey: Positive for tool, desire to see better account management and search capability, helped define our knowledge of which industries use Pro and how.

In-person interviews: Revealed more nuanced info. Two strong themes were we needed to help customers manage their users/teams and see their usage of Pro. We figured out that we needed a tiered system with roles and permissions, as well as make usage stats more accessible.

Exploration

Information architecture

Uncovered basic structure that would work well for accounts (owner, more hands off; team lead, does actual acct management; agent, does actual searches in tool.

Team exploration

This was a large undertaking that would ultimately affect every level of the Pro team, from sales to product to marketing. To get ahead of any issues that might crop up due to lack of information or communication, we decided to try a page out of the Google Design Spring and include the larger team in brainstorming. While mapping out flows and requirements, we also held a session with the Seattle team and later the Budapest team.

From the sketches, I began to build prototypes for InVision and flesh out profiles.
