Design Process

This project was one of the Pro product's more process-intensive UX changes at the time. It threaded into several large-scale projects like the move to SSO (single sign-on), Developer API launch, and a viral marketing campaign moving targeted users from consumer site to Pro.

Original settings

The initial scope was a reskin to match our new branding. Screens below give an overview of Account Settings, Site Administration, and Subscription Plans for purchase.

I also had designed the new Pro API Developer portal. While clean and approachable, we needed to find a more seamless way to integrate it with the rest of our Pro services.

First attempt

While giving the account settings page a refresh, we discovered deeper usability issues. Important existential questions came up like:

  • "Who uses all these sections?"
  • "Who on the acount manages users?"
  • "What do users actually do in account settings?"
  • "What do first time users expect to do when they go into it?"
  • "What don't they use in it?"

Step back - Research

Interviews with customers and internal users of Pro revealed areas that needed further analysis, which we pursued with surveys and in-person interviews. I also went along to sales and customer meetings with the lead PM to ask specific usability questions and watch users interact in real-time.

Survey:
  • Very positive reviews for product's utility
  • Desire for better account management and search capability
  • Gave us clearer insight into industries and usage
In-person interviews:
  • Revealed more nuanced info
  • Need to help customers manage their users/teams with roles and permissions
  • Also need clear view into usage statistics for larger teams

Exploration

Information architecture

Between product and engineering, we held collaborative sessions to discuss goals for the account overhaul, as well as potential engineering scope. We defined a basic account structure that would work well for different businesses:

  • Agent—Performs daily searches in Pro
  • Team lead—Pro account management and searches
  • Owner—More hands off, likely handles payment

With that info, I mapped out our collective research and conclusions to see what would make sense for users.

Team exploration

We opened up ideation to the larger Pro sales and marketing team, since this was a large undertaking that would ultimately affect them as well. We took a page out of the Google Design Sprint to present them with the research and brainstorm as a group, holding a session in Seattle then in Budapest.

Iterate with wireframes

I began to build paper prototypes in InVision to flesh out journeys for each of our potential user profiles. This helped us define areas that would overlap or be unique to each experience.
