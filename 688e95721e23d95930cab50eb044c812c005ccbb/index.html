Amazon Payments wanted to serve a greater swathe of shoppers by accepting more payment methods—like cash—during 2017's rapid company expansion.

This complex project was the first of its kind in the now larger, freshly restructured org. As lead designer, I spearheaded integrating UX into Consumer Payments and broadly working across other major teams (like Your Orders and Returns) at Amazon.

My task was to incorporate this method in the byzantine, sparsely-documented Amazon.com checkout processes. I worked closely with product, engineering, and an external research firm to iterate towards a simple yet global solution.

Pro Accounts

Tiers of angel

Desktop
Product

Whitepages Pro (now Ekata Mastercard)

Goal & Audience

Evolve account management for growing number of B2B identity verification customers

My Role

Work closely with PM to define and implement changes

Whitepages Pro was expanding its B2B customer base and needed to upgrade its account management UX.

Among the top reported pain points were limitations in user management and reviewing usage. Additionally, we needed to add developer toolkits for the API. Working closely with the product manager and a scrappy engineering team from Budapest, we dove deep to solve the problem across industries and use cases.

As the sole designer, I conducted research with the PM by visiting businesses, gathering data, iterating on solutions with usability, and honing in on a more robust account information architecture that would be durable for the future.
