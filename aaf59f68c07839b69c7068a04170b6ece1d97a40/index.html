Amazon PayCode

Cash is still king

Desktop, mobile, web
Product

Amazon Payments—Global transaction processing

Goal

Accept cash for purchases

Audience

International & US shoppers that want access to Amazon.com goods despite lack of access to credit cards

My Role

Lead designer from PRFAQ to launch for desktop, mobile, web

It looks a little better for Amazon for my future that I can pay with cash... Interviewee, Brazil, 2018

Amazon Payments org wanted to serve a greater swathe of shoppers by accepting more payment methods—like cash—during 2017's rapid company expansion.

It may seem surprising for e-commerce, but physical currency still has a strong influence on purchasing behaviors across the world (and it's considerably cheaper to process than credit cards). Many countries like Brazil, Mexico, and Indonesia already have voucher-like payments enmeshed in their economy, both offline and online.

I was tasked with integrating this complex method into the byzantine workings of Amazon.com's checkout process. The project was the first of its kind in the now larger, freshly restructured org. It served as a guinea pig for UX integration into Consumer Payments, as well as broadly working across other major teams at Amazon.

Study Shift

Academic personal trainer

iOS
Product

TASL—Education support for non-traditional undergrads

Goal

Boost academic success of new students via free mobile apps

Audience

American students (mostly over 24) that—in some combo—work full time, have dependents, and/or attend school part-time

My Role

Design iOS apps from scratch & monitor analytics

Thanks for creating this! It's kept me a lot more accountable so far. Survey response, Mar. 2017

All Star Directories sought to build a suite of mobile apps that support new students and improve institutional retention rates.

For adults returning to school, relearning how to be a student is a daunting obstacle on top of life's other demands. Drop-out rates for the first semester alone are the highest. TASL's ambition was to improve outcomes for new students through their first quarter, first year, and hopefully to graduation.

In 2016, I got a chance to design Study Shift—a study tracking app—and Reading Comprehension—an app that teaches and reinforces critical reading skills—from the ground up. With just 3 people, our team ran like a hackathon and made incredible progress within 6 months.
