Homepage

The need to rethink our homepage became apparent in our design challenges as we tried to incorporate the new navigation onto it. Our team played with concepts earlier that year in an exercise to modernize the site and get it in line with our brand guidelines. This felt like the perfect opportunity to test those explorations.

With our early concepts already available, we started by bringing together stakeholders to make our case and discuss goals and feasabile opportunities on the page.

We asked ourselves a few questions as we began to think of what should belong on the homepage. Rather than place content for the sake of having, we discussed pros and cons of adding or removing information. We decided to focus on these areas to shape our decisions:

  • Clear call to action for search
  • Download/awareness of other services like mobile apps
  • Incremental to rebrand that was already under way

This was a rather large change that was counter to our usual end metric of revenue. With brand and UX focused changes, the metric tend to be harder to collect and more subjective. We settled for better page stats like engagement and bounce rate.

With these points in mind, I worked with another designer to try a few iterations. Between ourselves, we wittled our ideas down to one idea each and gave feedback to each other on our direction. Once we were satisfied with the presentability, we met with key stakeholders again.

The top concern was that ever-present tradeoff between a strong and authoratative brand and ad revenue. In our iterations, we designed with that in mind. For testing, we had the option to go all-in, but to calculate revenue trade-offs, we developed designs that stepped through the company's tolerance for removing ads.

As we closed in on the final design and figured out where we needed to make compromises, our front-end developer created a version to split test.

We started testing the variation at a lower than usual percentage since this was such a drastic change, and we collected data over the course of a month. Bounce rate dropped and time on page increased compared to our control. Longer term, subjective benefits wouldn't become apparent until much later on.
