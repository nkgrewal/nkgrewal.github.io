LeadsCon was the first foray into rebranding efforts with the new logo. The company had a loose idea of what direction they wanted to go, and this event was the impetus to shape it even further. Coordinating with business development, we decided that the messaging needed to convey to current and potential partners that All Star had established its priority as being a lead provider of quality and consistency in the industry, and therefore a key to success.

With this in mind, I created a design that would stand apart from the overly glossy ads of our competitors. They tended to lean on trendy, web 2.0 designs and stock imagery. THE DISCLAIMER

I feel it's important to clarify immediately that this video is not meant to pick a side or incite a particular call to action. My intent was to explore the nature of conflicts in general using Isreal and Palestine as an example.

PATTERNS OF WAR
AND PERSECUTION

Its cyclical nature and the speed of escalation can be found in numerous examples both present and past, from Darfur to Myanmar to Iraq. By juxtaposing the present and the past, I attempted to make those observations palpable to the audience.

THE CONUNDRUM

Expressing the above in video with limited assets.

PROCESS

The original idea behind this project was to explore the dynamic between visuals and sound in the form of a music video. The exercise involved setting free music from renderyard to a collection of random footage from the Prelinger Archives.

While I continued to research and collect video in December '08, the ceasefire between Israel and Palestine was broken, and the Gaza War began. Al-Jazeera announced that they were releasing their footage online under Creative Commons licensing, which I leveraged to compare to the older videos I found on Prelinger. The first scenes are from a tourist view of Damascus and Jerusalem in 1936. From there, the footage transitions into scenes from Sands of Sorrow (which I really recommend seeing the entirety of). As the camera follows the road that many refugees had walked fleeing old Palestine to Gaza, I tried to create a transition of time as well as place. The roads, the people, the makeshift homes, they are different but the same. Over 50 years later, the next generation has grown only knowing this reality, and I tried to address that.
